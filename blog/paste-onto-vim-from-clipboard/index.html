Paste to Vim from the System Clipboard

PUBLISHED ON JUL 11, 2019 — PRODUCTIVITY, QUICK TIPS

Ever tried to paste something you just copied from your browser or from anywhere else on your system onto vim? As long as you still have a mouse with a middle button, all is well, but what happens when you are on a laptop or are using a smart mouse of some sort? Depending on your system configuration that could become a very challenging task.

This article explains the issues and solutions to this in great detail. Here I am only interested in one solution, i.e. how to paste onto my vim document what I just copied using the good old CTRL+c command.

In short, one has to use the + register, which points to the system clipboard register (this should work on all unix-like systems), with this vim command: "+p.
You are welcome. :-P
